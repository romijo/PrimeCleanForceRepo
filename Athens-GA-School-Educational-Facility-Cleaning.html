The Hidden Cost of "Good Enough" Cleaning

Why generic janitorial services fail in educational settings.

In a densely populated environment like a school, pathogens spread rapidly. "Visual cleanliness" (no trash on the floor) is not the same as "hygienic cleanliness" (no pathogens on surfaces). Research from NSF International reveals that the most contaminated areas in schools are often overlooked by standard cleaning crews.

The "Presenteeism" Problem

When students or teachers come to school sick, they contaminate shared surfaces within hours. A strategic cleaning plan breaks this chain of transmission, directly reducing absenteeism rates in Athens-Clarke County schools.

Comprehensive Facility Protocols

Our systematic approach ensures no surface is left untouched. We divide educational facilities into distinct zones, each with a tailored hygiene protocol.

Zone 1: Academic & Admin Areas (Classrooms, Libraries, Offices)

Focus: reducing distraction and surface transmission.

  • High-Touch Disinfection: Daily sanitizing of desk edges, door handles, light switches, and shared keyboards.
  • Whiteboard & Tray Care: Removal of ghosting and marker dust accumulation.
  • Trash & Recycle: Emptying bins and replacing liners; spot-cleaning bins to prevent odors.
  • Floor Care: HEPA-vacuuming carpets to trap allergens; damp mopping hard floors with neutral cleaner.
  • Dusting: High-dusting vents and shelves (above 6ft) to improve air quality.
Zone 2: High-Risk & Sanitary Areas (Restrooms, Nurse's Offices)

Focus: Pathogen elimination and odor control.

  • Hospital-Grade Disinfection: Utilizing EPA-registered disinfectants with appropriate dwell times.
  • Fixture Sanitation: Scrubbing and polishing faucets, flush handles, and dispensers.
  • Grout & Partition Care: Deep cleaning grout lines and stall partitions to remove bacteria buildup.
  • Supply Restocking: Ensuring soap, towels, and toilet tissue are fully stocked for the next day.
  • Nurse's Station: specialized terminal cleaning protocols for recovery cots and waiting areas.
Zone 3: Common & High-Traffic Areas (Cafeterias, Gyms, Hallways)

Focus: Safety, slip-prevention, and large-scale sanitation.

  • Cafeteria Tables: Cleaning and sanitizing tables and chairs (NSF-approved chemicals).
  • Water Fountains: Daily disinfection of spigots and buttons (critical vector point).
  • Gym Floors: Dust mopping and auto-scrubbing to maintain traction and prevent slip-and-falls.
  • Locker Rooms: Heavy-duty disinfection of benches, lockers, and shower floors to prevent MRSA/Athlete's Foot.
  • Entrance Mats: Vacuuming and arranging mats to trap dirt before it enters the building.
